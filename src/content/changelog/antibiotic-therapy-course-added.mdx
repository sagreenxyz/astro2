## What's new

The **Antibiotic Therapy in Nursing Practice** course is now live. It equips BSN
students with the knowledge and clinical reasoning skills needed to administer
antimicrobials safely, recognize signs of treatment failure or adverse reactions,
and apply antibiotic stewardship principles across care settings.

### Highlights

- Classification of major antibiotic families by mechanism of action and spectrum of activity
- Pharmacokinetic principles of antibiotic dosing (time-dependent vs. concentration-dependent killing)
- Evidence-based antibiotic stewardship strategies to reduce resistance
- Culture-guided therapy: interpreting sensitivity reports and collaborating with the care team
- Nursing considerations for common adverse effects, including allergy, nephrotoxicity, and C. difficile
- Patient education on adherence, side effects, and the dangers of antibiotic misuse
- Alignment with AACN Essentials (D1, D2, D4, D6, D9), NCLEX-NG, and QSEN competencies (PCC, EBP, S, I)
